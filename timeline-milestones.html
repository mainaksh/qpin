Our Roadmap to 2040

Qpin Systems is on a mission to develop a 100-qubit fluxonium-based Fault-Tolerant Quantum Computer (FTQC) by 2028, achieving a Quantum Volume ≥ 2^20 and ≥50 Algorithmic Qubits (AQu). With an initial ₹3,000 Crore investment, we will scale to 500 qubits by 2030, 10,000+ by 2035, and 100,000 by 2040, securing ₹30,000 Crore through milestone-driven phases. Our roadmap is backed by partnerships with MIT, Caltech, Harvard, Waterloo, ETH Zurich, Oxford, Stanford, and Maryland, ensuring technical and scientific precision.

2025-2026: Phase 1 - 100-Qubit Genesis (₹3,000 Crore)

  • Prototype Development: Fabricate 100 fluxonium qubits with EJ/EC = 5-20, EJ/EL = 20-100, achieving T1 ≥ 1 ms and 1Q gate fidelity ≥ 99.998%.
  • Wafer-Scale Fabrication: Implement 300 mm wafer-scale production with electron beam lithography (EBL < 5 nm) and Josephson junction uniformity (σ/μ ≤ 1.5%).
