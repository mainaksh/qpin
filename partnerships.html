Our Strategic Alliances

Qpin Systems has forged partnerships with the foremost global institutions in quantum computing to accelerate our mission of developing a 100-qubit fluxonium-based Fault-Tolerant Quantum Computer (FTQC) with ≥50 Algorithmic Qubits (AQu) by 2028, scaling to 100,000 qubits by 2040. Why Our Partnerships Matter: Collaborations with MIT, Caltech, Harvard, and others guarantee access to Nobel-caliber expertise, accelerating innovation and maximizing ROI in trillion-dollar markets.

Our Partner Institutions

MIT Center for Quantum Engineering

Based in Cambridge, USA, MIT leads in quantum computation and information science. Home to researchers like Seth Lloyd, MIT contributes expertise in quantum algorithms and error correction, supporting our QLDPC decoding and AQu ≥ 50 goals.

Caltech Institute for Quantum Information and Matter

Located in Pasadena, USA, Caltech is renowned for quantum information science, led by John Preskill, who coined "quantum supremacy." Their expertise in qubit design aids our fluxonium architecture (EJ/EC = 5-20, EJ/EL = 20-100).

Harvard Quantum Initiative

In Cambridge, USA, Harvard advances quantum science and engineering. Their work on quantum simulation supports our applications in molecular dynamics for drug discovery and artificial photosynthesis.

University of Waterloo Institute for Quantum Computing

Based in Canada, Waterloo is a global leader in quantum technology, contributing to quantum algorithms and hardware. Their expertise supports our GraphQEC/Astra decoding (<500 μs latency).

ETH Zurich Quantum Center

In Switzerland, ETH Zurich excels in quantum engineering and computation. Their advancements in superconducting qubits enhance our cryo-CMOS systems for 10x wiring reduction.

University of Oxford Quantum Computing and Devices Group

In the UK, Oxford pioneers quantum computing hardware, led by experts like Ilana Wisby. Their work on scalable quantum systems supports our 300 mm wafer-scale fabrication with EBL < 5 nm.

Stanford Quantum Computing Lab

In Stanford, USA, the lab focuses on quantum hardware and algorithms. Their contributions to quantum control systems bolster our fault-tolerant architecture (p_L < 1e-6).

University of Maryland Joint Quantum Institute

In Maryland, USA, JQI leads in quantum information and simulation. Their expertise in quantum error correction supports our QLDPC/4D codes, ensuring scalability to 100,000 qubits.
